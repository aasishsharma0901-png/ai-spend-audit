# Business Model

AI Spend Audit is designed primarily as:

```txt id="jlwmk5"
a lead-generation and qualification product
```

rather than a standalone paid SaaS tool.

The primary monetization path comes from:

* AI infrastructure credit sales
* Credex consultation bookings
* vendor partnerships
* long-term infrastructure relationships

The audit experience helps identify:

* high-intent users
* oversized AI spend
* infrastructure optimization opportunities

# Funnel Requirements

To reach ~167 customers within 18 months:

| Stage                  | Estimated Requirement |
| ---------------------- | --------------------- |
| Consultation bookings  | ~420                  |
| High-savings audits    | ~2,800                |
| Total completed audits | ~7,000                |
| Total visitors         | ~17,500               |

This averages roughly:

* 1,000 visitors/month initially
* growing toward ~2,000 monthly visitors by month 18

This growth target appears achievable through:

* consistent content distribution
* organic sharing
* founder communities
* SEO-driven comparison traffic
* public share links

# Critical Assumptions

The business model depends heavily on:

```txt id="jlwm77"
high-savings audit frequency
```

If:

* AI tooling becomes cheaper
* vendors consolidate pricing
* overlapping subscriptions decline

then:

* average detected savings may shrink
* consultation conversion rates may decrease
* monetization efficiency may weaken

This is considered the largest long-term business risk.

# Why the Funnel Could Work

The product benefits from:

* low friction onboarding
* fast time-to-value
* shareable outputs
* highly relevant pain points
* strong startup curiosity around AI spend

Unlike traditional procurement tools, the audit can deliver perceived value in:

```txt id="jlwm04"
under 2 minutes
```

which significantly improves completion likelihood.
